Abraham Lincoln

The man who freed slaves in American history.


Here's a time line of Abraham Lincoln's life:



  • 1809 - Born in Kentucky on February 12, 1809
  • 1827 - Earned his first dollar, ferrying passengers to steamboats on the Ohio River
  • 1836 - Licensed to practice law
  • 1846 - Nominated to be the Whig candidate for U.S. Congress and elected in the same year
  • 1848 - Gave a speech on floor of the House against Polk's Mexican-American War policies
  • 1861 - Inaugurated as first Republican president and gives his inaugural address
  • 1863 - Issued the Emancipation Proclamation on September 22 during Civil War, which frees slaves in certain Southern jurisdictions
  • 1865 - Re-elected and delivered his Second Inaugural Address in Washington, D.C.; got killed, at the age of 56, by John Wilkes Booth at Ford's Theatre on April 15.
  • 1876 - A gang of counterfeiters attempted to steal Lincoln's body but failed.
  • 1897 - Abraham Lincoln Memorial university was established at Harrowgate in East Tennessee
  • 1901 - Robert Todd Lincoln ordered that his father be buried under several tons of concrete to insure the body will not be disturbed again
  • 1909 - His image was placed on the one-cent piece in honor of the centennial of Lincoln's birth
  • 1914 - Lincoln's face is placed on the first five-dollar Federal Reserve Bank Note
